About Our School

Founded in 1943 by Ven. Balangoda Ananda Maithreeya Thero, our school stands as a proud center of education and moral excellence in the heart of Balangoda. For over eight decades, we have guided generations toward knowledge, discipline, and compassion.

Our School History

In the early 20th century, English was considered a language of prestige, used mainly by the educated elite. However, the people of Balangoda had no access to an English education, creating a social divide within the community. To bridge this gap, the Venerable Balangoda Ananda Maithreeya Thero recognized the need to establish an English-medium school for local children.

On 5th July 1943, with the blessings of the townspeople, the Thero founded the school under the name “Miriswatte Sri Dharmananda English School”. The first classes were held in a humble hut built from wattle and daub, roofed with coconut thatch, and attended by twenty students.

The Venerable Ananda Maithreeya Thero himself served as the first manager, while Mr. K.Y. Rambukwella was appointed as the first principal. In 1946, the simple classroom was replaced with a permanent building approximately 80 feet in length.

On 1st May 1945, the school was registered as a Junior Secondary School and handed over to the government. Later, on 27th June 1952, a new building constructed by the government was officially opened.

Following 1958, the school entered a period of remarkable progress — establishing new facilities such as science laboratories, sports grounds, and Advanced Level classes, along with the creation of a Cadet Corps and various student organizations.

In 1978, the school was renamed R/Balangoda Central College, and later achieved National School status on 22nd December 1993 under the principalship of Mr. E.D.S. Ananda Siriwardena, whose leadership brought significant academic and infrastructural growth.

In 2002, the institution was officially renamed Balangoda Ananda Maithreeya Central College, honoring its revered founder. Since then, successive principals, including Mr. M.G. Ranasinghe, Mr. R.M. Yasarathna, Mr. Anura Ekanayake, Mrs. D.S. Mallikarathna, and Mr. G.R.W.D. Silva, have continued to develop the school in both physical infrastructure and academic excellence.

Today, Balangoda Ananda Maithreeya Central College stands as a proud National School — a beacon of discipline, wisdom, and compassion, guiding generations of students to build a better future.

Our Revered Founder – Ven. Balangoda Ananda Maithreeya Thero

The Ven. Balangoda Ananda Maithreeya Thero was one of Sri Lanka’s most respected Buddhist scholars and educationists. Guided by deep compassion and foresight, he believed that true education must unite knowledge, virtue, and service.

In 1943, he founded the Balangoda Ananda Maithreeya Central College with the noble aim of offering English-medium education to rural children, thereby opening pathways of equality and opportunity. His efforts inspired generations to pursue both academic excellence and moral discipline.

The Thero’s vision continues to shape the heart of our institution — nurturing students who embody wisdom, mindfulness, and humanity. His name remains a guiding light for our entire school community.

True education is the awakening of inner wisdom.

Ven. Balangoda Ananda Maithreeya Thero
